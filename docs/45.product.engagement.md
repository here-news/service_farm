## The Core Insight

We've built a sophisticated epistemological system. But it needs to connect to human motivations.

**The challenge**: Most news engagement today is passion-driven (outrage, tribal identity) which leads to misinformation. We want to channel passion toward truth-seeking.

---

## What People Actually Want

| Passion (Emotional) | Rationality (Cognitive) |
|---------------------|-------------------------|
| To be RIGHT | To understand what's true |
| To be FIRST | To predict outcomes |
| To MATTER (agency) | To avoid being fooled |
| To BELONG (tribe) | To make good decisions |
| To feel SMART | To see through complexity |
| To WIN | To have reliable information |

**Current news exploits PASSION** (outrage, tribal identity).

**We channel passion toward TRUTH.**

---

## Models That Merge Both

| Platform | Passion | Rationality | Result |
|----------|---------|-------------|--------|
| **Prediction markets** | Betting, winning | Evidence-based | Truth-seeking gambling |

| **Stack Overflow** | Reputation, status | Best answer wins | Expert meritocracy |

Each succeeds by making **being right feel good** while requiring **evidence to win**.

## Risks and Mitigations

### Risk: Gamification Corrupts Truth

Concern: Users game the system for rewards, not truth.

Mitigation:
- Only independent corroboration rewards
- Source credibility weighting
- Contradiction detection penalizes false consensus
- Reputation tracks accuracy, not volume

### Risk: Competition Creates Tribalism

Concern: Users form teams that compete for "their truth."

Mitigation:
- Rewards tied to resolution, not position
- No "team" structure
- Evidence quality matters more than volume
- Entropy reduction benefits everyone

### Risk: Early Signal Gaming

Concern: Users submit low-quality early claims to get badges.

Mitigation:
- Early signal only counts if event becomes significant
- Stake required for submissions
- Low-quality claims burn stake
- Accuracy track record matters
